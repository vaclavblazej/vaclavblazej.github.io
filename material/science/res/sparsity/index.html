Sparsity DocCourse Prague 2018:

» work in progress «

Sparsity DocCourse ↗ was held between November 4. and December 15. in Prague on MFF UK, organized by Jaroslav Nešetřil with help of Matěj Konečný, Jan Hubička. The course took 6 weeks and so the majority of speakers and attendees were present only for a small part of the course. On fourth week there was a three day trip to Český Krumlov. The lectures were spread throughout the week days, usually 2 lectures a day; in total there were about 36 lectures.

The course was preceded with a small introducation into model theory as part of the standard course on mathematical methods.

Speakers were: Zdeněk Dvořák ↗, Jaroslav Nešetřil ↗, Daniel Kráľ ↗, Patrice Ossona de Mendez ↗, Marcin Pilipczuk ↗, Michał Pilipczuk ↗, Sebastian Siebertz ↗, Pierre Simon ↗, Blair D. Sullivan ↗, Szymon Toruńczyk ↗, Felix Reidl ↗, Jakub Gajarský ↗.

Themes

  • Shallow minors
  • Polynomial expansion
  • Bounded expansion
  • Nowhere dense
  • Model checking
  • Sparseness and stability
  • Limits of sparse graphs
  • Neighborhood complexity
  • Interpretations
  • Kernelization on sparse graphs
  • Complex networks and sparsity
